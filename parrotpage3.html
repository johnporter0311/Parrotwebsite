Reasons not to get a parrot

Reasons not to get a parrot

Parrots cost a lot of money. Acquiring a parrot from a shelter can cost up to $800 or more. Acquiring a parrot from a breeder can cost as much as $3000 or more. This does not include cages, toys, or food.

Life spans for parrots can be a problem. Most parrots have a real possibility of out living their owners. Macaws can live up to 50 years old or more. African Grays and Cockatoos tend to live past 50 years old. Bird rescues see a lot of parrots that come in from households where their owners have passed away.

High maintenance. Parrots are extremely high maintenance. Parrots require a lot of time and the more you have the more time that is required. On average you would need to spend a few hours a day with each bird but it also depends on the bird. Each bird is as different as people. Having a parrot is like having a 3 year old that never grows up.

The pain of owning a parrot. Like any pet, parrots bite. Parrots bite for numerous reasons. In most cases it is because you did something they do not like. Sometimes they want to play, they are hungry, they may not like you, they might bite because they are scared, or they may be just looking to get a reaction from you. Regardless of the reason, a bite from a parrot hurts a lot. The larger species of parrot have the ability to remove a finger with a bite. They do not care where they bite. It can be a finger, hand, arm, foot, leg, or face. So if you like scares, then by all means, get a parrot because if you have a parrot long enough you will get bit.

Aggression of some parrots can be bad. Especially if you are not expecting it. They may have had a bad experience with a human. For example a person with a beard may have abused the parrot. That parrot will show fear and aggression to every person with a beard. Some parrots may show aggression toward men or women in general. Most parrots will be sweet but show aggression when in their cage. They live there and will aggressively defend that home. Basically, always assume the parrot is aggressive until the parrot proves otherwise.

Mating season can certainly add to the aggression. Parrots become aggressive and unpredictable. Depending on the breed of parrot breeding season can last months. For example, Cockatoos have two seasons. During this time they are completely unpredictable and could bite for no reason. Even if the parrot does not have a mate or other parrots around. The hormones are there. A wrong touch or if the parrot is pet incorrectly can trigger aggression when their hormones are peaking.

Noise is a parrots middle name. Parrots are extremely loud by nature. Parrots have morning chatter, daily chatter, happy outbursts, alarm calls, mating calls, evening greetings, and bedtime calls. A 747 Jumbo Jet produces as much as 140 decibels of noise. 105db is considered damaging to the ear. Peacocks have been registered at 115 db. Cockatoos hold the record as the loudest bird on earth at 135db. So try having a conversation or taking a nap with that kind of noise.

Sizes of parrots vary. Parrots can range in size from about 3.5 to 40 inches and weigh 2.25 to 56 ounces, on average. The world's heaviest type of parrot is the kakapo, which can weigh up to 9 lbs. The smallest parrot is the buff-faced pygmy parrot, which is only about 3 inches tall and weighs just 0.4 ounces. They size of their cage is extremely important. As a general rule, the cage should be at least one and a half times larger than the parrots wing span. That can lead to tight quarters in a lot of homes for people especially if they have more than one bird. Not having the proper sized cage can cause destructive behavior such as aggression or plucking.

Respiratory systems of parrots are very sensitive. Their respiratory system is highly sensitive to smells. Items such household cleaners, candles, perfumes, and air fresheners can make a parrot very sick quickly or kill them with no warning. Imagine paying $3000 for a parrot, another $1000 for the cage, and hundreds for food and toys only to light a candle or use a spray cleaner in your kitchen and have your parrot fall over dead moments after using the item.

Parrots like people have mental issues. Depression can be caused by several things. Not socialized enough, the wrong food, abuse, dirty cage, etc.. Parrots can and will pluck their own feathers. Feather plucking can occur in cases where birds are severely depressed and unsettled in their environments.

Destroying things is second nature for parrots. Parrots are destructive. This ability to destroy things is in their nature. But if the bird is suffering from depression or anxiety, then it is amplified. Parrots like to naturally chew which leads to that destructive power. You can buy a $100 toy and it will be reduced to splinters in seconds. Accidentally let them near window blinds, telephone cords, window sills, walls, flooring, etc.. and you could be looking at thousands in repairs. This mess leads to the next part.

Messy and parrots go hand in hand. Destroying your favorite shoes or destroying your walls can leave a mess. But other aspects of leaving a mess must be considered. Parrots poop a lot. They can build up towers of poop quickly. Although most can be potty trained, if they have to go then they do not care where. Unfortunately they like to throw their food. It is amazing how far they can throw it when they want to. Some parrots, such as Cockatoos, have a dust. This dust helps make their feathers water proof. If they are not bathed often that dust builds. It comes off on you and your clothing when you handle them. It can also affect the respiratory system of other parrots. This dust can get into your home heating and cooling system and affect your respiratory system.

Routine is key to a parrot's life. Parrots like routine. Awakening at a certain time, feeding at a certain time, playing and interacting at certain times, and going to bed at a certain time. As we know life can screw with routines. As people we generally adapt and overcome. Parrots do not care. They do not adapt to new things well or quickly. This causes depression, and anxiety which leads to destructive attitudes towards themselves or others. So if your routine changes a lot. Then a Parrot is not for you.

Movies, and television shows have done the parrot world a great disservice. According to these media devices parrots say anything you want and on cue. This is nowhere near the truth. Yes parrots can mimic sounds they hear over and over but it is seldom on cue. In fact, some parrot species do not speak in a language we understand. This belief that parrots speak regularly is often why parrots are abused when they do not speak on cue or are turned over to a rescue because they are no longer wanted as a novel status symbol.
